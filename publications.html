1. A. Dupré, S. Mylvaganam, “A simultaneous and continuous excitation method for high-speed EIT with reduced transients and noise sensitivity“, Sensors, Volume 18, Issue 4, 1013 (2018) [link]

    This publication reports on a novel experimental protocol/system architecture for electrical impedance tomography based on the concept of Frequency-Division Multiplexing. A single continuous excitation that is the linear superposition of multiple sinewaves at different AC frequencies is set at all electrodes. Suppressing transient effects from contact impedance and reducing noise sensitivity of impedance estimates are the main benefits of the proposed method. A proof-of-concept experiment has been set up to illustrate the method with a simple four-electrode sensor.
  2. A. Dupré, G. Ricciardi, S. Bourennane, “Novel Approach for Analysis and Design of High-speed Electrical Impedance Tomographic System for Void Fraction Measurements in Fast Two-phase Flows “, IEEE Sensors Journal, Volume 17, Issue 14, pp. 4472-4482 (2017) [link]

    This publication reports on the development of a prototype electrical impedance tomography (EIT) sensor. The sensor features high frame acquisition rate, up to 6000 fps in classic adjacent mode, and up to 800 fps in the novel full scan mode. The repeatability of the experimental measurements is comparable with other reported high-speed EIT systems. A new calibration procedure based on the full scan measurements efficiently assess electrode-specific impedances caused by electrode misplacements or contact impedance. An analysis of the spectral behavior of impedance measurements highlight the influence of contact impedance at current-injection electrodes.
  3. A. Dupré, G. Ricciardi, S. Bourennane, S. Mylvaganam , “Electrical Impedance Based Flow Regimes Identification - Multiphase Experiments and Sensor Modelling“, IEEE Sensors Journal, Volume 17, Issue 24, pp. 8117-8128 (2017) [link] This publication concerns the development of a methodology for flow regime identification using electrical capacitance tomography (ECT). The sensor and the rig operated for generating a database of flow measurements are in University College of Southeast Norway. The originality of the method lies in the analysis of the raw signal of the ECT sensor, and a data fusion technique based on the eigenvalues of the capacitance matrix.
  4. A. Dupré, “Electrical Impedance Tomography for Void Fraction Measurements of Harsh Two- phase Flows: Prototype development and Reconstruction techniques”, PhD thesis manuscript, Ecole Centrale Marseille, France (2017) [link]

    This Ph.D. thesis manuscript reports on various aspects of the development of a prototype highspeed electrical impedance tomography sensor at CEA Cadarache. The R&D project is motivated by the need of reliable void fraction estimates of high temperature high pressure two-phase flows.
  5. A. Dupré, A. Vasiliev, H. Ferroukhi and A. Pautz, “Towards Modeling and Validation Enhancements of the PSI MCNPX Fast Neutron Fluence Computational Scheme based on recent PWR Experimental Data”, Annals of Nuclear Energy, Volume 85, pp. 820-829 (2015) [link]

    This publication concerns the results of my M.Sc. thesis and neutronics simulations for nuclear reactors. The reactor pressure vessel (RPV) is the most critical component of the nuclear power plants regarding safety. Predicting aging of the RPV requires a source term: what is the flux and energy of neutrons at the RPV? In this publication, starting from reactor core calculation results and power history of a Swiss reactor, transport simulations with the probabilistic Monte-Carlo code MCNP have been performed. A methodology with weight-windows and source biasing has been defined to increase the computational efficiency of the numerical scheme.
