Main Publications

    The publication compiles main findings of the CRADL EU Horizon 2020 project.

  2. A. Dupré, K-M. Lei, P-I. Mak, R.P. Martins, W.K. Peng, "Micro- and nanofabrication NMR technologies for point-of-care medical applications – A review", Microelectronic Engineering, Volume 209, pp. 66-74 (2019) [link]

    A Review Paper about NMR technologies for point-of-care medical applications I have authored during my post-doctoral research fellowship in INL Portugal.

  3. A. Dupré, S. Mylvaganam, “A simultaneous and continuous excitation method for high-speed EIT with reduced transients and noise sensitivity“, Sensors, Volume 18, Issue 4, 1013 (2018) [link]

    This publication reports on a novel experimental protocol/system architecture for electrical impedance tomography based on the concept of Frequency-Division Multiplexing. A single continuous excitation that is the linear superposition of multiple sinewaves at different AC frequencies is set at all electrodes.

  4. A. Dupré, G. Ricciardi, S. Bourennane, “Novel Approach for Analysis and Design of High-speed Electrical Impedance Tomographic System for Void Fraction Measurements in Fast Two-phase Flows “, IEEE Sensors Journal, Volume 17, Issue 14, pp. 4472-4482 (2017) [link]

    This publication reports on the development of a prototype electrical impedance tomography (EIT) sensor featuring high frame acquisition rate (up to 800 fps in the novel full scan mode) and high repeatability.

  5. A. Dupré, “Electrical Impedance Tomography for Void Fraction Measurements of Harsh Two- phase Flows: Prototype development and Reconstruction techniques”, PhD thesis manuscript, Ecole Centrale Marseille, France (2017) [link]

    This Ph.D. thesis manuscript reports on various aspects of the development of a prototype highspeed electrical impedance tomography sensor at CEA Cadarache. The R&D project is motivated by the need of reliable void fraction estimates of high temperature high pressure two-phase flows.

  4. A. Dupré, G. Ricciardi, S. Bourennane, S. Mylvaganam , “Electrical Impedance Based Flow Regimes Identification - Multiphase Experiments and Sensor Modelling“, IEEE Sensors Journal, Volume 17, Issue 24, pp. 8117-8128 (2017) [link] This publication concerns the development of a methodology for flow regime identification using electrical capacitance tomography (ECT). The sensor and the rig operated for generating a database of flow measurements are in University College of Southeast Norway. The originality of the method lies in the analysis of the raw signal of the ECT sensor, and a data fusion technique based on the eigenvalues of the capacitance matrix.

  5. A. Dupré, G. Ricciardi, S. Bourennane, “Identification of flow regimes using raw EIT measurements”, Conference WCIPT8, Foz do Iguacu, Brazil (2016)

    This peer-reviewed conference paper describes flow regime identification methodology developed during my PhD thesis. It has been subsequently published in a special issue of IEEE Sensors Journal.

  6. A. Dupré, G. Ricciardi, S. Bourennane, “Development of a prototype device for imaging two-phase flows via electrical impedance tomography”, Conference SWINTH, Livorno, Italy (2016)

    This peer-reviewed conference paper describes preliminary results and design of the prototype EIT sensor developed during my PhD thesis.

  7. A. Dupré, G. Ricciardi, S. Bourennane, “On the feasibility of retrieving phase indicator function of a bubble flow in an electrical impedance tomography experiment”, Conference ISPT8, Dresden, Germany (2015)

    This peer-reviewed conference paper discusses feasibility of retrieving phase indicator function in bubble flow and helped structure planning of my PhD thesis

  8. A. Dupré, A. Vasiliev, H. Ferroukhi and A. Pautz, “Towards Modeling and Validation Enhancements of the PSI MCNPX Fast Neutron Fluence Computational Scheme based on recent PWR Experimental Data”, Annals of Nuclear Energy, Volume 85, pp. 820-829 (2015) [link]

    This publication concerns the computation of the neutron flux that reach the Reactor Pressure Vessel for predicting aging. Starting from reactor core calculation results and power history of a Swiss reactor, transport simulations with the probabilistic Monte-Carlo code MCNP have been performed. A methodology with weight-windows and source biasing has been defined to increase the computational efficiency of the numerical scheme.

  9. A. Dupré, A. Vasiliev, H. Ferroukhi and A. Pautz, “Extension and validation enhancements of the PSI Fast Neutron Fluence MCNPX based computational scheme using recent KKG experimental data”, Master thesis manuscript, Paul Scherrer Institute, Villigen, Switzerland (2014)

    This MSc thesis manuscript presents the development of a methodology for neutron fluence computation and main results of the comparison with experimental data.

  10. A. Dupré, A. Vasiliev, H. Ferroukhi and A. Pautz, “Validation enhancements of the PSI fast neutron fluence computational scheme using operational PWR experimental data”, Student conference of the American Nuclear Society, Pennstate University, USA (2014)

    This peer-reviewed conference paper describes neutron fluence computational methodology developed during my MSc thesis. The article was awarded the prize for best paper in Reactor Technology by the organising committee.
